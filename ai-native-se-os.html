The Architecture

Three layers, one system

Every SE organization faces the same scaling trap: bespoke quality doesn't scale, and scaled approaches don't feel bespoke. I designed, built, and governed a connected three-layer infrastructure to solve it — letting a team of 16 SEs produce high-quality, tailored work across the full deal cycle without sacrificing the judgment that makes SE valuable.

Layer 01
Knowledge & Workflow Base
Structured deal documents, live CRM connections, and templates with defined fields. The single source of truth every downstream output reads from. Every hour invested here multiplied the quality of every output downstream.
→
Layer 02
AI Skill Library (~20 Skills)
~20 purpose-built skills — each covering one job at one deal stage. Skills chain together: the output of one becomes the input to the next. Human review gate at every step. Covers discovery, qualification, proof, proposal, and expansion.
→
Layer 03
Dynamic Delivery Layer
Branded client-facing deliverables generated from Layer 1 data. The SE edits the data; the document re-generates on demand. No design tool, no reformatting. Final SE review gate before anything reaches a client.
The Governance Model

What the system owns.
What the SE owns.

An SE team won't trust a system that makes invisible decisions. The AUTO/SE discipline was the thing that made this system adoptable — not just good design. Clear ownership at every step is the governance model.

AUTO
System-populated
CRM fields, metrics from product analytics, account research, document structure, first-draft competitive framing, ROI scaffolding, standard objection responses. The AI is faster and more consistent than a human at this work — and it should own it entirely.
SE
Human-authored
Discovery diagnoses, the narrative arc that explains why this solution for this buyer, the commercial path recommendation, the escalation call when a POC is at risk, and the final review gate before anything reaches a client. The system exists to protect SE time for exactly this.
Design Principles

What I learned building it

01
Structure before automation
Every hour spent on clean document templates before writing a single skill multiplied the quality of every output. Messy inputs produce messy outputs regardless of how good the AI is. The single most important investment in this system was making the knowledge base airtight before anything else.
02
The governance model is the product
An SE team won't trust a system that makes invisible decisions. The AUTO/SE discipline was the thing that made this adoptable — not just good design. SEs need to know exactly what the system owns and what they own. Ambiguity in governance means the system gets abandoned.
03
Every deal deposits into the system
Discovery insights, objection patterns, what champions said in their own words, what moved deals forward — all of it feeds back into future deals. The system in year two is materially more capable than year one. Compounding is the real ROI.
04
The SE's job is judgment — protect that
The system exists to give SEs more time for diagnosis, relationship-reading, and the moments where you set the script aside. Whatever we automate, we cannot automate what makes SE valuable. If the system starts to feel like it's replacing judgment rather than freeing it up, that's a signal to recalibrate.
